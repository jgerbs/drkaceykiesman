Ideal For

  • General wellness

How It Works

A customized blend of vitamins and minerals is mixed and delivered directly into the bloodstream. Because it bypasses the digestive system, it can offer higher absorption than oral supplements. A doctor will review your history, do a quick exam, and customize your drip before treatment begins.

Details

Treatment Details

Time

Most IV treatments take 30–75 minutes, depending on the type of infusion and the vitamins included. IM injections are approximately 15 minutes.

Downtime

None. Most patients can return to normal activities right away unless their provider advises otherwise.

Customization

Your IV can be tailored to your goals, with wellness-focused blends and add-ons used where appropriate based on your assessment.

Safety Screening

Before treatment, your health history is reviewed to make sure the infusion is safe and appropriate for you.

Comfort

Most people feel only a small pinch when the IV is inserted, then relax comfortably while the infusion runs. You are monitored throughout.

Before Your Visit

We recommend eating a light meal and drinking water before your appointment to help you feel more comfortable during the infusion.

FAQ

Common Questions

What does IV vitamin therapy help with?

IV therapy may help support hydration, energy levels, recovery from illness or travel, and general wellness. Please note that IV vitamin therapy is currently under-studied and is not a proven treatment.

Who should avoid IV therapy?

IV therapy may not be recommended for people with kidney disease, heart failure, severe allergies to any of the vitamins used, or certain chronic health conditions. Your provider will review your history before treatment and determine if IV therapy is safe for you.

Can I eat before my IV?

Yes. We recommend eating a light meal and drinking water before your appointment to help you feel more comfortable during the infusion.

Are there any side effects?

Side effects are uncommon but may include mild bruising at the IV site, feeling cold during the drip, and temporary lightheadedness. Serious reactions are rare, and you are monitored throughout the treatment.

Is IV therapy covered by MSP or insurance?

IV vitamin therapy is considered a private service and is not covered by MSP. Some extended health plans may cover treatments provided by a naturopath. We offer direct billing to most insurance companies.

Do I need a consultation first?

Yes. Your first visit includes an assessment where a doctor reviews your needs and creates a personalized plan. Treatment may begin the same day if appropriate.
