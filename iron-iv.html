What to Expect

About Iron Infusions

Results

  • Improved energy levels and reduced fatigue
  • Correction of iron deficiency more efficiently than oral supplements

Ideal For

  • Iron deficiency or iron-deficiency anemia
  • Those who cannot tolerate or absorb oral iron
  • Post-surgical or postpartum iron depletion
  • Chronic fatigue linked to low iron levels

How It Works

Iron is delivered directly into the bloodstream through an IV, bypassing the digestive tract for full absorption. The infusion runs slowly over approximately 60-90 minutes while you are comfortably monitored. A consult and bloodwork review are required before your first infusion to confirm suitability and determine the appropriate iron dose and preparation.

Details

Treatment Details

Consult First

A pre-iron IV consult is required before your infusion to ensure suitability. Bring any existing bloodwork or have it sent in advance. Additional labs may be ordered if needed.

Iron Ordering

Once your consult is complete and iron has been prescribed, it will be ordered to the pharmacy. The pharmacy will then contact you for payment, please look into your insurance plan to see if you have coverage available. Once payment is complete, the iron will be sent to our clinic, and your infusion appointment can be booked.

Duration

The infusion appointment is 105 minutes to allow extra time for slower infusion if needed. Iron is administered slowly to minimize the risk of adverse reactions, and you are monitored throughout.

Downtime

Most patients can resume normal activities the next day. Some may experience mild fatigue, joint pains or a headache after the infusion, which typically resolves within 72 hours.

Safety Monitoring

You will be monitored during and after the infusion for any signs of reaction. Your health history is reviewed at the consult to ensure the treatment is safe and appropriate for you.

Before Your Visit

Eat a light meal and stay well hydrated before your infusion appointment to help with comfort during your IV. Avoid arriving on an empty stomach.

FAQ

Common Questions

Do I need bloodwork before my IV?

Yes. A pre-iron IV consult is required before treatment. If you have existing bloodwork, please send it in prior to the visit. If additional labs are needed to confirm suitability, they will be ordered at that appointment.

How long does an iron infusion take?

The iron IV therapy appointment is 105 minutes. This allows time for the infusion to be administered slowly and safely, and for monitoring during and after treatment.

How soon will I feel better after my IV?

Individual results vary depending on the severity of deficiency. Many patients may start to notice improvements in energy and fatigue within 1–2 weeks, with full iron stores typically replenishing over 6–8 weeks.

Who is iron IV therapy for?

Iron IV therapy is suitable for people with confirmed iron deficiency or iron-deficiency anemia. Lab testing is required to confirm your suitability, and is ordered at your pre-iron IV consult (unless you have recent labs within 3 months).

Are there any side effects?

Most people tolerate iron infusions well. Mild side effects may include temporary nausea, headache, or discomfort at the IV site. You will be monitored throughout the infusion. Serious reactions are rare but possible, and your provider will review your history before proceeding.

Is iron IV therapy covered by MSP or insurance?

Iron IV therapy is a private service and is not covered by MSP. Some extended health plans may provide partial coverage for iron when purchased at the pharmacy, and the iron administration fee can be submitted to extended benefits under naturopathic services. We offer direct billing to most insurance companies.
